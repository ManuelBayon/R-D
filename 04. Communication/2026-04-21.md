  
*MDL = Market Data Layer*

## 1.1 Responsibilities
 
- The MDL ingests a `MarketDataEvent` at every `step` of the backtest.
- The MDL maintains an (internal) market state that is updated exclusively via `ingest(event)` and is exposed as read-only.
- The MDL exposes the most recently ingested `MarketDataEvent`.
- The MDL exposes a window of the last `n` consecutive `MarketDataEvent`s.

---
## 1.2 Assumptions and Prerequisites

- `BacktestEngine` injects a `MarketDataEvent` at each step.
- Each `MarketDataEvent` has a non-empty `event_id` of type `str`.
- Each `MarketDataEvent` has a `timestamp` of type `datetime`.
- Each `MarketDataEvent` has a `bar` field of type `OHLCV`.
- All `MarketDataEvents` pertain to a single `instrument` and a single `bar_size`.

---
## 1.3 Ownership

- The MDL holds the market state, which is represented by a `list[MarketDataEvent]`.
- The only component authorized to call `ingest()` is the `BacktestEngine` orchestrator.
- No write access to `_history` is exposed externally.

---
## 1.4 Invariants

- The timestamp of each ingested `MarketDataEvent` is strictly greater than that of the previous one.
- The history is append-only (no deletions, modifications, or intermediate insertions).
- The order of ingestion is preserved.
- The last element of the history always corresponds to the last ingested `MarketDataEvent`.

---
## 1.5 API Specification

- `latest()` returns the most recently ingested `MarketDataEvent`.
- `window(n)` returns the last `n` ingested `MarketDataEvent`s, in the order they were ingested.
- `latest()` == `window(1)[0]`.

---
## 1.6 Errors

- `MarketStore.ingest(event)` raises `BacktestInvariantError` if `event.timestamp <= last.timestamp`.
- `latest()` raises `ContextNotInitializedError` if no events have been ingested.
- `window(n)` raises `ValueError` if `n <= 0`.
- `window(n)` raises `ValueError` if `n > len(history)`.

---
## 1.7 Out of Scope

- It does not load data from an external source
- It does not sort or reorder market events and assumes that their order is guaranteed by the upstream
- It does not produce decisions or executions.
